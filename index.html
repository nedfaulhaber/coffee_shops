The Goldilocks Coffee Finder

Get the Coffee Experience That's Juuuuust Right




There are a LOT of coffee shops in Portland - which is best for you? Answer the questions below and find out...





How important is it that a shop is conveniently located and easy to find?




When getting your favorite coffee drink, do you want it to taste the same each time you drink it?




Do you want your coffee shop to have a Wi-Fi network?




Would you ever put caramel, vanilla, pumpkin spice, or other such flavorings in your coffee?





We Recommend:




Starbucks

525 NE Grand Avenue (503) 231-2793 starbucks.com

The Seattle-based, omnipresent coffeehouse chain can be credited with starting the trend in the US towards the Italian-style cafe with espresso drinks, way back in 1983. Since that time, there are over 26,000 of these shops around the world, so if consistency is what you are looking for, you will get it at Starbucks, and it will not be hard to find one close by. If you like to dress up your espresso, this is your place, with caramel, whip cream, peppermint, pumpkin, chocolate, and more ready to tantalize your taste buds. When it comes to lavish, decadent lattes, nobody really competes with Starbucks.

Dutch Bros.

4721 NE Columbia Boulevard (503) 282-0029 dutchbros.com

This western US chain of drive-through coffee stands, founded in Grants Pass, OR, has a rabid following, often nicknamed the 'Dutch Mafia', who are absolutely wild about the friendly baristas, super-fast service, and fun vibe. It is not uncommon to hear loyal customers say that their experience at Dutch Bros is the best part of their day because the energy is so positive and up-beat. It is a young crowd, with a decidedly youthful enthusiasm, but any ages are welcome.Drive-thru chain known for specialty java and espresso drinks, plus teas and smoothies.

Woodlawn Coffee and Pastry

808 NE Dekum Street (503) 954-2412 woodlawncoffee.com

Located in a beautiful, century-old building at the burgeoning triple intersection (The Woodlawn Triangle) of Dekum, Durham, and NE 8th Ave, this cozy coffeehouse also makes delicious baked goods uses seasonal, organic ingredients. The interior is warm and old-timey, with wood floors and tables and an oldsaloon-esque bar. They serve stellar local coffee from Coava and Roseline roasters, as well as teas from Tao of Tea. A true neighborhood feel will put you at ease and feel like part of the family.




Barista

1725 NE Alberta Street (503) 208-2568 baristapdx.com

Barista is in the heart of the popular Alberta district of NE Portland, offering carefully prepared and presented coffee drinks from a variety of excellent roasters. The baristas behind the beautiful long bar are top-notch, so you can expect high quality espresso here. The laid back atmosphere is punctuated by the quirky decor that gives the place a 19th century, old-timey feel.




The Fresh Pot

3729 SE Hawthorne Boulevard (503) 232-8928 thefreshpot.com

This is a quaint little cafe branch of the local mini-chain featuring loose-leaf teas, WiFi, a small library and local roasts. The fact that it is attached to Powells Books on Hawthorne gives it a classic bookish feel, a place you could hang out and read for awhile. The decor is simple and no-nonsense, with a row of comfy little tables along the wall and a few with a great look out the window for people watching on the busy Hawthorne strip.




Either/Or

8235 SE 13th Avenue (503)235-3474

Either/Or is a bright, cheery little space in the Sellwood neighborhood presenting locally roasted coffee, espresso flights, handmade sodas and pastries. The baristas are some of the best in a city known for its great baristas - this place is highly touted internationally and the baristas have won top awards for their latte art and drink making. Befitting its name, Either/Or offers two daily choices of single-origin espresso from stellar local roasters such as Heart and Roseline, as well as signature, seasonal coffee cocktails. They are also responsible for making the wonderfully spicy Tanglewood chai, which one can find at many shops around town. This is a place for the coffee aficionado.




The Dragonfly Coffee House

2387 NW Thurman Street (503) 224-7888 thedragonflycoffeehouse.com

Cozy, open-early spot pairing its brews with pastries and more in a low-key space with plush seating, dolls, and pillows. It is a magical atmosphere, full of red, warm hues and lamps, vases, chandeliers, and potted plants. A small library of books, a stack of games and puzzles, and fun art on the walls make it an entertaining place to be. The large LOVE sign behind the bar befits the welcoming vibe of the baristas and in a sense defines this little neighborhood spot.




Sterling Coffee Roasters

417 NW 21st Avenue (503) 248-2133 sterlingcoffee.com

This tiny (VERY tiny) coffeehouse in the heart of the bustling Alphabet district features high quality, carefully crafted coffee drinks with their own varying roasts, which include a traditional espresso roast and a rotating single origin offering. The classic decor, with its white table-clothed small tables, small flower arrangements, gilded mirrors, and golden green wallpaper, give this place a European feel, somewhere between France and Italy. Besides the stellar coffee, their hot chocolate, using cocoa made from local artisan chocolate maker Cacao, is the top-rated in town.




Fehrenbacher Hof

1225 SW 19th Avenue (503) 223-4493 goosehollowinn.com

This family-run coffeehouse in the cute, residential Goose Hollow neighborhood offers breakfast sandwiches and homey touches like plush chairs, couches and chess tables. Located in a beautiful old house, this place is the picture of cozy comfort, with lots of tiny corners to disappear into, as well as a porch where you can relax on warm days. Known by its regularsas'The Hof', it is a hidden gem of Portland.




Heart Coffee

537 SW 12th Avenue (503) 224-0036 heartroasters.com

The western outpost of these highly-renowned, innovative local roasters serves top-notch, carefully crafted espresso drinks for the true aficionado. Its industrial-chic quarters are stark but welcoming, and the bar along the window is a great place to sidle up for some laptop work or reading. The bustling indoor atmosphere and high volume music makes the place feel alive, but you can also opt for a quiet, modern designed table outside on the sidewalk. Heart is fast becoming a local landmark in a city renowned for its excellent coffee.




Anna Bannanas Cafe

8716 N Lombard Street (503) 286-2030 annabannanascafe.com

Laid-back is the key word at Anna Bannanas - the sign outside says to come in and “catch a breath”, while the owners are the self-proclaimed “champions of chill”. It’s got an eclectic, bohemian assortment of comfy furniture, with a lived-in quality that is the absolute opposite of industrial-chic. The framed picture of Jerry Garcia is a clue to the old hippie vibe that subtly inhabits the place, as does the whimsical artwork on the old brick walls and the little castle for kids to play in. Though the place is usually crowded, its got a lot of space to accommodate them.




Cathedral Coffee

7530 N Willamette Boulevard (503) 935-4312 cathedralcoffee.com

A bit off the beaten path of downtown St Johns, Cathedral Coffee is a hidden gem that serves carefully crafted, beautifully poured espresso drinks and quality coffee. The interior is a combination of simple and bohemian, traditional and modern, hip and down-home. The huge mural depicting graffiti tags and the St Johns bridge anchors them as a solidly neighborhood spot that gives the feeling of being in the heart of St Johns, even if you’re not. The coffee here is top- notch and will impress any aficionado out there - and well worth the trip up north.
